## What is the model?

The model is a tall stack of identical layers. Your text enters as **tokens**, each
token becomes a **vector**, and those vectors flow upward through the stack. At every
layer two things happen: **attention** lets each token gather context from the others,
and a **feed-forward network** does the per-token "thinking." Residual connections and
normalization keep the signal stable across dozens of layers.

The signature mechanism is attention: every token decides, with varying weight, how much
to listen to every other token. That is how context flows.

<DeeperBlock summary="The attention score">
Attention compares each query against every key and normalizes the result:
$\text{Attention}(Q,K,V) = \text{softmax}\!\left(\frac{QK^\top}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right)V$.
The $\sqrt{d_k}$ keeps the scores from saturating the softmax as dimension grows.
</DeeperBlock>

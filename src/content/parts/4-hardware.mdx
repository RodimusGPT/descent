## How does the silicon run it?

At the bottom of the descent is the GPU. Thousands of **tensor cores** chew through matrix
multiplications; a steep **memory hierarchy** feeds them, from vast-but-slow HBM to
tiny-but-fast on-chip SRAM. The art of fast inference is keeping the cores fed.

One idea explains most of the behavior: **arithmetic intensity** — how many calculations
you do per byte you move. Prefill is compute-bound and saturates the cores. Single-stream
decode is memory-bound and starves them. Batching raises intensity and reclaims the
wasted compute. Below this lies only electrons.

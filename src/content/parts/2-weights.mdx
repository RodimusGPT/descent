## How are the weights represented?

Zoom into the model far enough and the abstraction dissolves into **numbers** — billions
of them, arranged in matrices. "Running" the model is mostly multiplying those matrices.

So the question becomes physical: how do you store one number? A 32-bit float is precise
but heavy. Sixteen bits halves the cost. Quantization pushes further — eight, four, even
two bits per weight — trading a little accuracy for a lot of memory. The representation
you choose decides whether a model fits on your GPU at all.

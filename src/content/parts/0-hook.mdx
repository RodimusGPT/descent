You typed something and hit enter. A fraction of a second later, words appeared — and it felt
like the machine *understood* you. What actually happened is stranger, and more mechanical.

### One token at a time

The model did exactly one thing, very fast, over and over: it read everything so far and
predicted the **most plausible next token** — a whole word, or a fragment of one. Then it
appended that token to the text and did it again, and again, until it picked a token that means
*stop*.

Pick a prompt and predict its next token. Everything below this point — the transformer, the
weights, the serving stack (the software that runs the model), the silicon — exists to answer that
one question, fast: *given everything so far, what comes next?* You'll meet each of them on the way
down.
